Fire Sprinklers


We evolved fire safety around you by taking three step.

Fire Sprinklers

Wet Pipe: 

  • Water is always present in the pipes that supply the sprinkler system and sprinkler heads.
  • When triggered the air is released followed by water to suppress the fire.
  • Used in areas where the temperature drops to freezing and crack the pipes.
  • Ideal for underground garages, walk-in fridge/ freezers.
  • The most common system in industrial and commercial systems.
  • Used indoor where the risk of water freezing is non-existence.

Dry pipe: 

        
  • Water is held back by a control valve.
  • When triggered the air is released followed by water to suppress the fire. 
  • Used in areas where the temperature drops to freezing and crack the pipes.
  • Ideal for underground garages, walk-in fridge/ freezers.
  • Air is always present in the pipes that supply the sprinkler system and sprinkler heads.

Pre-Action:

  • Similar to a hybrid of the dry and wet system.
  • Water is held back by a control valve.
  • In order to fill the pipes with water, a smoke alarm, or heat alarm should be triggered. Hence the term pre-action.
  • If the piping system has a leak, the filling of water can be stopped, also ensure undesired water leak from pipes does not occur.
  • Used in Locations where accidental activation is undesired.
  • Ideal for Library, museums, Data Centers, Computer labs etc.

Deluge and recycling:

  • An open system where the pipes are empty and unpressurized.
  • There is no valve element at sprinkler heads.
  • The trigger to activate the system can be manual or from other sensors.
  • Usually controlled by fire alarm panel.
  • Covers larger area.
